Much like the rolling hills of the Ozarks, the NWA music scene has its highs and lows. Sometimes we output magnificent music that astonishes the world — other times, we sit stagnant in poser city while the DIY venues struggle to scrape together enough pennies to compete with the Walmartian Bagillionaires.

As a fourth-generation resident Arkansan, William Lonon decided he wanted to do something about the lack of unified connective tissue surrounding the local culture. The scene had the talent. It had the energy. What it lacked was infrastructure — a unified hub that connects artists together, and a label that actually gave a damn about the artists it worked with, understood the culture it came from, and wasn't trying to sand down the rough edges to make something more palatable for an algorithm.

Therefore, with the help of his trusty dusty compooter, dozens of cans of Monster energy (not sponsored), a couple subscriptions to your favorite LLMs, and a $20,000 spare-change (student debt) investment into a music industry data science degree, he put his ass to work.

Octoquan Records launched in 2026 — an independent label built to give weird music a home, Arkansas artists a platform, and the underground a little more connective tissue. No major label money. No gatekeepers. No playlist algorithms running the show (well, maybe a few). Just good music pushed hard into the world through whatever means necessary.

From the founder

"Octoquan is the culmination of many hard nights full of procrastination, angst, passion, Eureka Pizza (regrettably), and sheer expression of free will. If you are so inclined as to hop on the wagon, send your demos to my submission inbox, where I will put them on and consider them for inclusion into my domain of funky tunes. What we stand for

01

Artist Ownership

You own your masters. Full stop. We are a platform, not a landlord. Every deal we make puts the artist in control of their work, their image, and their future. The music belongs to the person who made it — always.

02

Unapologetically Weird

Genre requirements: none. If it's honest, if it's got something to say, if it makes someone feel something — we want to hear it. The weirder the better. We exist for the music that doesn't fit anywhere else.

03

Rooted in Arkansas

Built in the Ozarks, operating everywhere. We exist to strengthen the connective tissue of the NWA grassroots scene and push its music out into a world that hasn't heard it yet.

how

works.

01

Submit Your Demo

Send us your music through the demo submission form. No industry connections required, no genre gatekeeping. If it's real, we want to hear it. We listen to everything that comes in.

02

We'll Be in Touch

If your music is a fit, William will reach out directly. No form letters, no intermediaries. Just a real conversation about what you're making and what working together could look like.

03

You Keep Your Masters

This is non-negotiable. Every artist on the Octoquan roster owns their recordings outright. We help with distribution, promotion, and getting your music in front of people — we don't take your work in exchange.

04

Get It Out There

Once you're on the roster, your music goes everywhere — all major streaming platforms, physical distribution where it makes sense, and whatever scrappy DIY promotion we can throw at it. Arkansas and beyond.
